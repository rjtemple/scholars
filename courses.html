Introduction to Data Science

Jackson Temple

Foundations of data science from three perspectives: inferential thinking, computational thinking, and real-world relevance. Given data arising from some real-world phenomenon, how does one analyze that data so as to understand that phenomenon? The course teaches critical concepts and skills in computer programming and statistical inference, in conjunction with hands-on analysis of real-world datasets, including economic data, document collections, geographical data, and social networks. It delves into social and legal issues surrounding data analysis, including issues of privacy and data ownership.

Introduction to Economics

Daphne Feijoo

This survey course offers a broad overview of the field of economics, making it accessible to students new to the subject. It introduces key economic concepts, such as supply and demand, market structures, and the role of government in the economy. Students will gain a foundational understanding of microeconomics, which examines how individuals and businesses make decisions, and macroeconomics, which focuses on national and global economies. The course also touches on topics like economic growth, inflation, unemployment, and international trade, providing a solid framework for further study in economics.

Introduction to Linear Algebra

Sahil Malhotra

This course covers the foundational principles of linear algebra and differential equations, both of which are crucial for advanced study in mathematics, physics, engineering, and computer science. Topics include matrix arithmetic, determinants, and vector spaces, which form the building blocks for solving systems of linear equations. Students will explore inner product spaces, eigenvalues, and eigenvectors, which are used in everything from machine learning to physics. The course also examines linear second-order differential equations and systems of first-order differential equations with constant coefficients, providing practical applications for modeling real-world phenomena. Fourier series are introduced, offering a method for analyzing periodic functions.

Introduction to Microbiology

Samuel Skolnick

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to the fundamentals of biology, covering topics from the microscopic to the macroscopic. Students will explore the structure and function of cells, gaining an understanding of organelles and cellular processes essential to life. The course delves into molecular and organismal genetics, discussing how genetic information is stored, transmitted, and expressed in living organisms. In addition, students will study animal development, learning how organisms grow from a single cell into complex, multicellular entities. The course also covers form and function in biology, analyzing how structure at the molecular and organismal levels supports life processes.

Introduction to Computer Science

Jackson Temple, Samuel Skolnick

This course introduces key concepts in computer science, focusing on abstraction techniques to manage program complexity. Students will learn procedural, control, and data abstraction through recursion, higher-order functions, and object-oriented design. The course covers multiple programming paradigms, including functional and object-oriented, and provides an introduction to algorithm analysis. Several significant programming projects will give students hands-on experience, preparing them for more advanced topics in computer science.
